Glossary

Quick definitions of terms used across this site—covering navigation items, UI labels, and scientific vocabulary.

A

About
Page describing background, mission, and context for the site or organization.
Angiogenesis
Formation of new blood vessels—important for tissue repair and recovery.
Anabolic response
A physiological state that promotes building tissue (e.g., muscle growth, repair).
Anti-aging
Broad term for interventions aimed at preserving function or slowing age-related decline.

B

BPC-157
Research peptide often discussed for tissue repair and inflammation modulation.
Builder / Stack Builder
Interactive tool to select peptides and view combined benefits, side effects, and synergies.

C

CJC-1295
Growth hormone–releasing hormone analog discussed for GH secretion and fat metabolism.
Collagen synthesis
Production of collagen, a structural protein crucial for skin, joints, and connective tissue.

E

Epithalon (Epitalon)
Peptide investigated for telomere biology and longevity-related effects.

F

Fat metabolism
The body’s process of mobilizing and using fats for energy.

G

GH / Pulsatility
Growth hormone released in natural bursts (pulses) rather than steadily.
GHRP-6
Growth hormone–releasing peptide discussed for GH stimulation and metabolism.
GHK-Cu
Copper peptide associated with skin repair, hair growth, and tissue remodeling.
Glossary
This page—definitions for key terms used across the site.

H

HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)
Analytical method used to assess peptide purity and composition.
Hypertrophy
Increase in muscle size through growth of muscle cells.
Hair / Skin
Site categories related to cosmetic or dermatologic outcomes (e.g., elasticity, growth).

I

IGF-1 LR3
Long-acting insulin-like growth factor variant discussed for muscle growth and recovery.
In-Vitro testing
Laboratory experiments performed outside living organisms (e.g., cell cultures).
Ipamorelin
GHRP class peptide noted for GH release with minimal cortisol effect.

M

Mass spectrometry
Technique to determine molecular mass/structure; used to verify peptide identity.
Metabolism
All chemical processes in the body; often referenced regarding fat or energy use.

P

Peptide
Short chain of amino acids; smaller than proteins; often has targeted biological actions.
Pulsatility (see GH)
Pattern of release in bursts; commonly referenced for growth hormone.

S

Sermorelin
Growth hormone–releasing hormone analog used to stimulate GH release.
Stack / Stacks
Combinations of peptides designed to support a specific goal (e.g., recovery, fat loss).
Synergy / Synergies
When two or more agents together produce a greater or more comprehensive effect.
Skin elasticity
Skin’s ability to stretch and return to shape; linked to collagen and overall skin health.

T

TB-500
Synthetic peptide related to thymosin beta-4; discussed for healing and angiogenesis.
Telomere / Telomere protection
Chromosome end caps and efforts to preserve them, often associated with longevity research.
Tissue regeneration / Repair
Processes that restore damaged tissues, including rebuilding structure and function.
